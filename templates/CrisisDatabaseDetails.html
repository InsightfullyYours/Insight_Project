Database Details

The CDC WONDER AIDS Database is a wealth of information that should be explored in its entirety. Due to time constraints, this project looks at a specific subset of the data. This project focuses on the Location, Month of Diagnosis, Age at Diagnosis, HIV Exposure Category, and Vital Status. The Location provides the metropolitan area of the case and can be explored on this website. The Month of Diagnosis spans from January 1981 (the generally agreed upon beginning of the epidemic, as defined by collected data) through December 2002. The Age at Diagnosis spans from birth to 65+, in 13 age groups. The HIV Exposure Categories are:

  • Hemophilia/coagulation disorder
  • Heterosexual contact
  • IV drug use (females and heterosexual males)
  • Male homo/bisexual and IV drug use
  • Male homo/bisexual contact
  • Mother with HIV or HIV risk
  • Pediatric hemophilia
  • Pediatric receipt of blood
  • Pediatric risk not reported or indentified
  • Receipt of blood, blood components or tissue
  • Risk not reported or identified

The Vital Status code defines whether the patient was reported deceased before 2001 or not. (The cause of death is not included in the public database, so this report may include causes of death unrelated to the HIV/AIDS diagnosis). The Vital Status is not publically available after 2000 in order to protect the identities of patients from de-anonymization. The data has not been modified to account for age-linked mortality. This analysis is limited to cases in which all five categories are provided in the publically available database. For this reason, this analysis may under-count. The implications that are discussed are therefore limited to the data set as it is used.
